Did you know that almost every kilowatt-hour (kWh) of electricity you use has a hidden water cost? Water is required for cooling power plants, producing fuel, and generating electricity. This relationship is known as the Water-Energy Nexus.

üîé How Energy Production Uses Water

In a typical power plant, water is heated to produce steam. This steam turns turbines to generate electricity. Afterward, large amounts of water are used to cool down the system before it can start again.

üìà The Hidden Cost of Energy

When you switch on a light or use your computer, you are indirectly using water. Saving electricity is one of the easiest ways to conserve water.

üå± How to Save Water by Saving Energy

  • Switch to energy-efficient appliances (LED bulbs, star-rated devices).
  • Turn off lights, fans, and electronics when not in use.
  • Use public transport, cycle, or walk to save fuel (fuel production uses water).
  • Consider installing solar panels to generate water-free power.
üìä Quick Fact: Saving just 1 kWh of electricity can save up to 50 liters of water in water-intensive regions!
