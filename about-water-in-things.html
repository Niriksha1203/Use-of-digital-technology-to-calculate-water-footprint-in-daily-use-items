Cotton is one of the thirstiest crops on Earth. It needs huge amounts of water for irrigation, washing, dyeing, and finishing.

Leather production has one of the highest water footprints because:

  • Water is used to grow feed for cattle
  • Water is needed for the animals themselves
  • The tanning process uses large quantities of water and chemicals

One pair of leather shoes can require 8,000+ liters of water!

Electronics have a surprisingly high water footprint due to:

  • Water used to clean silicon wafers for chips
  • Cooling during manufacturing processes
  • Production of rare-earth metals

A single smartphone takes about 900 liters of water to produce. Laptops and large electronics use even more.
