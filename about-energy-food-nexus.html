The Energy-Food-Water Nexus

Water, food, and energy are deeply interconnected. This is called the nexus:

  • Water for energy – used in cooling power plants and growing biofuels.
  • Energy for water – used in pumping, treating, and distributing water.
  • Water for food – irrigation uses 70% of all freshwater withdrawals.

Saving one resource helps save the others. For example, eating less meat reduces both water and energy use.
